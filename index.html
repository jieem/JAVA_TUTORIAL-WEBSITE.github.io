Why was Java created?

Java was created to offer a platform-independent programming language capable of running on various devices and operating systems. Its development aimed to simplify software development while ensuring security, robustness, and reliability. Java's design prioritized platform independence, ease of use, and networking capabilities to meet the demands of the evolving digital landscape.

When did they create Java?

Java was created by James Gosling and his team at Sun Microsystems in the early 1990s. The official announcement of Java was made by Sun Microsystems on May 23, 1995. It aimed to provide a platform-independent programming language for simplified software development with a focus on security, robustness, and networking capabilities.

Who is the Developer of Java

Java was developed by a team of engineers at Sun Microsystems, with James Gosling playing a pivotal role in its creation. Gosling is often referred to as the "Father of Java" due to his substantial contributions to the language's design and development. Alongside Gosling, Mike Sheridan and Patrick Naughton were key members of the team responsible for Java's inception. Their goal was to create a platform-independent language suitable for building software for consumer electronics. Java's official release occurred in 1995, and it quickly gained popularity due to its simplicity, portability, and robustness.
